<p><ul><li>HPHT</li><li>CVD</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The synthetic diamond market comprises two primary types: High-Pressure High-Temperature (HPHT) and Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD). HPHT mimics natural diamond formation by applying extreme pressure and temperature, producing diamonds with characteristics similar to natural ones. CVD, on the other hand, involves a gas mixture that deposits carbon atoms onto a substrate, creating diamonds layer by layer. Both methods cater to various applications, including jewelry and industrial uses, driven by demand for cost-effective, high-quality synthetic diamonds.</p></p>
